Watch: Life after politics for former Edmonton city councillor Kim Krushell

Larry Wong, Edmonton Journal
Updated: November 7, 2018
Share Adjust Comment Print

Former Edmonton city councillor Kim Krushell, now president of a startup company named Lending Assist, a software company designed to handle the complexities of commercial lending to make transactions easier and faster between lenders and lawyers, talks about transitioning from politics to the business world.

“My family mattered and my husband’s (Jay) business mattered more, so that’s why I took the risk. And I turned down job offers that I did get … I decided … No, I’m going to do this no matter how much I’m not going to get paid to start” said Krushell. “The thing that was driving Jay crazy was the inefficiency just in general,” she said. “He was going, ‘Why can’t we do it faster? Why does everything have to be paper?’”
